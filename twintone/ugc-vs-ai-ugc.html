Choosing Between UGC and AI Actors

What is Traditional UGC?

Traditional UGC, or user-generated content, refers to content created by real customers, fans, or advocates - not by the brand itself. Think product reviews, social media posts, unboxing videos, testimonials, or customer photos. Essentially, it's the digital version of word-of-mouth, where genuine customer experiences are shared for others to see [10, 11]. This type of content lays the foundation for understanding how AI is reshaping UGC.

What makes traditional UGC so impactful is its authenticity. It's unscripted and created by everyday people, making it more relatable than polished brand content. Real customer experiences can validate a brand's promises in ways that traditional advertising simply can’t [11, 14].

The trust factor here is huge. For example, 47% of shoppers say user reviews on retailer websites are the most influential content when researching products - far outweighing the 11% who rely on branded social content. Plus, products with at least five reviews are 270% more likely to be purchased than those with none. As Laura DeGomez, Director of Partnerships & Brand Marketing at Well Traveled, perfectly sums up:

"No one tells our story better than our members."

Characteristics of Traditional UGC

What sets traditional UGC apart is its human element and personal connection. Unlike influencer content, it’s created purely from genuine consumer experiences, regardless of whether the creator has a large following [10, 2, 15].

This content often feels raw and unpolished - think honest reviews, casual social media posts, and spontaneous videos. It’s this unfiltered quality that makes it so relatable and trustworthy. In fact, 79% of consumers say UGC influences their buying decisions, and brands that incorporate UGC see 29% higher web conversions.

Some common formats include:

  • Written product reviews

  • Video testimonials

  • "Get Ready With Me" (GRWM) videos

  • Before-and-after transformations

  • Community forum discussions [18, 12, 6]

To encourage this kind of content, brands often reach out directly to their customers, run hashtag campaigns, host contests, or simply ask their community to share their experiences. These strategies help foster participation and keep the content authentic.

Challenges of Producing Traditional UGC

Despite its benefits, creating traditional UGC isn’t without its challenges. One of the biggest hurdles is time-consuming coordination. Brands have to monitor mentions, secure permission to reshare content, and maintain communication with creators - a process that can stretch over days or even weeks for just a handful of usable pieces [10, 13, 14].

Then there’s the issue of cost and inconsistency. While organic UGC is often free, professional UGC creators typically charge between $150 and $200+ for a 30-second video (as of 2025). Even with paid content, the quality can vary widely - from blurry photos to posts riddled with typos that don’t align with brand standards. Additionally, 53% of consumers prefer clear instructions on what to create, meaning brands need to invest time in setting guidelines and actively managing their communities.

Scaling traditional UGC is another tough nut to crack. Without active solicitation, high-quality organic content can be slow to surface. And coordinating with multiple creators across different time zones makes it nearly impossible to produce the volume of content needed for testing ad variations or launching time-sensitive campaigns.

AI UGC refers to AI-generated content designed to resemble traditional user-generated content (UGC) - often in video format. Instead of working with real creators, brands use AI-driven platforms to create videos featuring digital replicas, mimicking the casual, relatable style of organic social media posts.

While traditional UGC is based on real experiences, AI UGC relies on technology to craft similar content. As AKOOL explains:

"The goal is to replicate the authenticity and tone of UGC without relying on real influencers or customers."

This method blends the trustworthiness of UGC - 92% of consumers perceive it as more credible than standard advertising - with the efficiency and scale of AI. UGC vs AI UGC: Key Differences

When deciding between traditional User-Generated Content (UGC) and AI-generated UGC, the choice often boils down to five main factors: cost, speed, scalability, authenticity, and return on investment (ROI). Traditional UGC thrives on real people sharing genuine experiences, which builds trust but comes with higher costs and logistical challenges. On the other hand, AI UGC leverages technology to replicate user content at a fraction of the time and cost.

Cost is where AI UGC leads the pack. Traditional UGC involves higher production expenses, while AI UGC averages just $198 per video - a cost reduction of 44% to 77%. For instance, in 2025, a Shopify beauty store boosted its weekly content output from 5 to 25 posts (a 400% increase) while cutting production costs by 65%. This affordability makes AI UGC an attractive option for brands aiming to maximize their marketing budgets.

Speed and scalability are other areas where AI UGC excels. Traditional UGC can take 4–12 hours or even weeks to coordinate and produce, whereas AI UGC delivers polished videos in just 45 minutes - a time savings of up to 95%. This efficiency allows brands to test multiple messaging variations, translate content into different languages, and create personalized videos for various audience segments - all without the delays tied to human schedules.

Authenticity is where traditional UGC still shines. Real human experiences resonate deeply with audiences, which is why 84% of consumers are more likely to trust brands that use UGC in their marketing. Traditional UGC is seen as 2.5 times more authentic than brand-created content. However, AI UGC, despite becoming more advanced, faces skepticism - 46% of consumers report feeling uneasy about brands using AI vs. human influencers or synthetic content. As Maria Volina, Head of Marketing at Fourmeta, explains:

UGC isn't always as 'authentic' as it looks... with the rise of generative AI, the mirage is getting more convincing.

ROI presents a mixed picture. AI UGC often delivers stronger short-term results, especially for performance marketing. For example, in 2025, Dunkin' Donuts launched an AI-driven UGC contest, leading to a 57% spike in mobile app downloads - far exceeding their traditional contest benchmarks. AI UGC also drove a 25% to 35% increase in engagement rates on TikTok and Instagram that same year. However, traditional UGC is better suited for building long-term brand trust and emotional connections by leveraging synthetic relationships and engagement metrics, particularly for high-value or trust-sensitive products. Many successful brands combine the two approaches: AI UGC for rapid experimentation and scaling, and traditional UGC for core storytelling and deeper connections.

Advantages and Challenges of Each Approach

This section dives into the strengths and hurdles of both traditional and AI-generated user-generated content (UGC), offering a closer look at their trade-offs.

Advantages of Traditional UGC

Traditional UGC thrives on its ability to create genuine human connections. When real customers share their experiences, it resonates with audiences. And the numbers don’t lie - 93% of marketers agree that consumers trust content created by other customers more than brand-generated material. Plus, 79% of people say UGC strongly influences their buying decisions. This trust directly impacts revenue, with UGC-based ads achieving four times higher click-through rates and 50% lower cost-per-click compared to standard branded ads.

What sets traditional UGC apart is its raw, unpolished authenticity. The shaky camera work, unscripted moments, and less-than-perfect lighting make it relatable in a time when 71% of consumers are skeptical about what they see due to AI. As Rickie Sherman, UX Lead at Averi.ai, explains:

The shaky camera work, the genuine enthusiasm, the unscripted reactions… these aren't bugs, they're features.

This kind of "awkward authenticity" fosters emotional bonds and builds long-term loyalty. A great example is Frankies Bikinis, which showcased top-performing customer content on its site. The result? UGC accounted for 19% of total orders and over 23% of online revenue.

Another benefit of traditional UGC is the creative freedom it offers. Customers can make bold claims like "This changed my life!" - statements that brands are legally restricted from using in their own ads. Beyond promoting products, UGC creators often feel like they’re contributing to a community. As Chad Hetherington, Content Marketing Specialist, puts it:

UGC's sole purpose isn't just to boost brands... people post because they want to contribute to a community of like-minded folks.

However, this approach isn’t without its challenges, particularly when it comes to scaling and logistics.

Challenges of Traditional UGC

The biggest hurdle for traditional UGC is the logistics and cost involved. Creating this kind of content isn’t cheap - production costs range from $350 to $1,000 per video, and it takes 4–12 hours to produce. Add in the complexities of managing creators and coordinating schedules, and scaling this type of content becomes a real headache. For brands that need dozens of variations for different audiences or want to run rapid A/B tests, traditional UGC simply can’t keep up.

Advantages of AI UGC

AI-powered UGC builds on the strengths of traditional UGC by addressing its operational limitations. For starters, production is much faster and cheaper. Creating a video takes about 45 minutes - a time savings of 85% to 95% - and costs average just $198 per video. This efficiency enables brands to test multiple messaging variations, personalize content for different audiences, and even translate videos into various languages - all without waiting on human schedules.

Another major advantage is the level of creative control. Brands can fine-tune every detail, from visuals to messaging, ensuring everything aligns with their identity. Plus, with full ownership of the content, brands can skip the licensing and negotiation hurdles that come with traditional UGC. For example, the edtech startup Headway used AI tools to create localized video ads mimicking user testimonials. These ads generated 3.3 billion impressions and boosted ROI by 40% - all without hiring a videographer.

AI also excels at rapid iteration. Brands can quickly produce unlimited variations of content tailored to different platforms, demographics, and campaign goals. This approach has proven effective, with AI-driven UGC boosting engagement rates by 25% to 35% on platforms like TikTok and Instagram in 2025.

Challenges of AI UGC

The biggest challenge for AI UGC lies in trust and authenticity. While AI can replicate the look of user-generated content, it often falls short of capturing the human connection. In fact, 46% of consumers report feeling uneasy about brands using AI influencers or synthetic content. The "uncanny valley" effect - where content looks almost human but feels slightly off - can make viewers uncomfortable. As Maria Volina, Head of Marketing at Fourmeta, points out:

AI can mimic authenticity, but it can't fully recreate the human connection behind true UGC.

Transparency is another critical issue. Brands face increasing scrutiny from regulators, including FTC crackdowns on undisclosed AI endorsements and mandatory labeling laws in the EU and China. Without clear disclosures, such as hashtags like #AIgenerated, brands risk losing consumer trust and facing legal repercussions. Over-reliance on AI tools also brings the risk of creative fatigue, with ads starting to look so similar that audiences tune them out.

Generational differences further complicate the picture. While Gen Z spends more time on short-form videos and UGC than other groups - about 50 minutes more per day - only 24% trust AI tools offered by major brands. To address this skepticism, brands need to ground AI-generated content in real customer feedback or FAQs and intentionally include small imperfections to make it feel more genuine.

When to Use Traditional UGC vs AI UGC

Deciding between traditional and AI-generated user-generated content (UGC) depends on factors like trust, content needs, budget, and where your brand stands in its growth journey. The right balance comes down to your goals and brand maturity.

Here’s a general guideline: for early-stage brands, around 80% AI UGC works well for quick testing. Brands in the growth phase might go for a mix - 60% AI and 30% traditional UGC. Established brands often lean on about 50% traditional UGC to build trust, while using AI to scale content efficiently.

For high-ticket items like luxury goods or healthcare, traditional UGC is crucial to establish trust. On the other hand, lower-risk products can benefit from the speed and scalability of AI UGC. As Aastha Kochar from Invideo puts it:

Traditional advertising screams 'This is an ad.' Strategic UGC whispers 'This is a recommendation from someone like you'.

Use Cases for Traditional UGC

Traditional UGC is all about building trust and fostering long-term connections. It’s especially effective for new product launches. When customers are unfamiliar with your brand, seeing real people share authentic experiences builds the kind of social proof that overcomes hesitation. Take GoPro’s "Million Dollar Challenge" - they invited users to submit raw clips for a chance to win part of $1 million. The result? 42,000 submissions from 170 countries, 25,000 product transactions, and a conversion rate three times higher among those who engaged with the content.

This approach also works wonders for community-driven campaigns. For example, Petco’s "Spotted" program sent products to a select group of pet owners, achieving a 90% response rate and increasing review volume by 405%. This led to a 48% boost in revenue per visit for the featured products. Similarly, traditional UGC shines in influencer partnerships, where authenticity is key. Consider Rhode Skin’s early 2024 campaign: they gifted their "Lip Phone Case" to micro-influencers without any paid scripts. The result? $8.3 million in Earned Media Value and a 31.9% average engagement rate on Instagram.

Another strength of traditional UGC is its ability to provide diverse representation. Real customers showcasing various body types, skin tones, and lifestyles lend credibility that AI avatars just can’t replicate. It’s also the best option when you want creators to make personal claims, like "this changed my life", which brands themselves might be legally restricted from saying.

Use Cases for AI UGC

When speed, volume, and control are top priorities, AI UGC is your go-to solution.

AI UGC is unbeatable for rapid iteration and scaling content. Need to test multiple hooks or calls-to-action quickly? AI makes it possible to churn out dozens of variations in hours rather than weeks. GoPro, for instance, now produces half of its marketing videos using AI-curated UGC, doubling their output without adding to their team.

For brands with large product catalogs on platforms like Amazon or Shopify, AI UGC is a game-changer. Instead of coordinating hundreds of creators to showcase individual products, AI can generate consistent demos at scale. It’s also perfect for multilingual campaigns. A standout example is Malaria No More’s campaign featuring David Beckham speaking in nine languages using AI technology. This allowed them to deliver a unified message globally without requiring Beckham to learn new languages.

If you’re managing daily content across platforms like TikTok, Instagram Reels, or YouTube Shorts, AI UGC can help avoid delays that often come with human creators. At an average cost of $198 per video - compared to $350–$1,000 for traditional production - AI UGC is a budget-friendly option for brands needing frequent updates. It’s ideal for product demonstrations, feature tutorials, and seasonal campaigns where fresh content is needed quickly. Just remember to disclose AI-generated content to maintain transparency and comply with platform policies.

FAQs

How does AI-generated UGC affect consumer trust compared to traditional UGC?

AI-generated UGC has its perks, like quicker production and tailored content. However, it often misses the mark when it comes to the genuine feel that makes traditional UGC so relatable. Shoppers tend to trust the raw, unfiltered nature of real user content much more than polished, brand-created materials. In fact, studies consistently show that peer recommendations carry significantly more weight with consumers.

Over-relying on AI to produce or mimic UGC can backfire by damaging credibility and eroding consumer trust. The key is to use AI as a tool, not a replacement. When AI's speed and personalization are thoughtfully paired with authentic customer input, brands can create content that not only engages but also builds trust - leading to stronger connections and better returns.

How does AI-generated UGC save time and reduce costs for content creation?

AI-generated UGC is a game-changer when it comes to saving both time and money in content creation. Forget the hassle of organizing traditional photoshoots or video shoots - no more hiring creators, booking locations, or juggling complex production schedules. With AI, brands can generate top-notch images and videos on demand, slashing production timelines from weeks to mere minutes.

On top of that, brands have reported cutting 44% of their content creation costs by making the switch to AI-generated UGC. This means businesses can churn out the same amount of engaging, trust-building content at a fraction of the usual cost, freeing up budgets while still delivering high-quality results.

When should a brand use traditional UGC instead of AI-generated UGC?

Brands should opt for traditional user-generated content (UGC) when their campaign goals hinge on authenticity and building trust. Content created by real customers - like reviews, photos, and videos - connects with audiences on a deeper level because it feels honest and relatable, unlike AI-generated options.

Here’s when traditional UGC works best:

  • Boosting trust for significant purchases: For products like health and beauty items or big-ticket goods, authentic customer experiences can drive stronger conversions by showcasing real-world results.

  • Maintaining compliance and credibility: Real content steers clear of issues like deep-fakes or ethical concerns, making it especially valuable in tightly regulated industries.

  • Strengthening community bonds: Genuine customer voices foster a sense of belonging and inspire peer recommendations, which are essential for community-driven strategies.

When authenticity, trust, or compliance are essential, traditional UGC remains the go-to solution.
